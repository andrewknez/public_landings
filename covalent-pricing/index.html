FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

A licensed user is anyone with a training record or an administration role in the platform. That includes hourly operators tracked in the skills matrix, supervisors managing assignments, trainers signing off on evaluations, and admins configuring the system.

An LMS is built to deliver and track courses. Covalent is built to operate the workforce on the shop floor — planning coverage, sequencing on-the-job training, capturing trainer sign-offs, and gating job eligibility against live skill records.

Covalent complements your LMS rather than replacing it. Course completions from systems like Cornerstone, Absorb, or Docebo flow in as skill prerequisites, and Covalent layers on the OJT workflows, audit trails, and assignment logic that an LMS was never designed to handle.

Typical deployment runs 4–8 weeks depending on scope, data readiness, and integration complexity. Implementation covers platform configuration (organizational structure, roles, workflows), data migration (employees, training records, skill structures), administrator training, and SSO setup as needed.

All plans include tiered implementation and hypercare support depending on the plan. Pro and Enterprise customers also receive ongoing engagement from our customer success team to continuously roll out additional sites, objectives, and integrations.

Add users at any time at your contracted per-user rate. We true up at your next annual billing cycle rather than triggering a new contract every time you onboard new operators.

If growth pushes you past a tier threshold or warrants a tier upgrade, your data stays intact and our team can discuss activation of new features and contract amendments.

Data is stored in Covalent's AWS cloud with SOC 2 controls and access restricted to authorized parties. SSO is available on Pro (add-on) and Enterprise plans for centralized identity management.
