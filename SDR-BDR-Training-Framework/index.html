Understand the Company

Learn what Covalent is, why it exists, and how to explain it simply before you ever explain it to a prospect.

What Covalent does, in plain English

Picture a factory floor. Every job on that floor, running a machine, torquing a bolt, signing off on a part, needs someone who is trained and currently able to do it safely and correctly. Most factories still track this with paper checklists, binders, and messy spreadsheets. When a supervisor needs to know who can do what, they have to guess or go dig through paperwork.

Covalent replaces all of that paper and all of those spreadsheets with one live system. It keeps track of who is trained, who is qualified right now, who is about to expire, and who is actually available on shift, all updated in real time.

The one-liner. Use this any time someone asks "what does Covalent do?"

"We help industrial companies manage training, qualification, and assignment of work at scale. We replace paper, Excel and tribal knowledge with a modern system to track who's qualified to work on what."

Follow it with the proof: we ramp new operators about 30% faster and save each worker around 10 minutes of unproductive time per shift, which adds up to roughly $300K per line per year in savings. Covalent currently manages qualifications for 50,000+ workers across its customer base.

How Covalent thinks about its building blocks

You will hear these words constantly in meetings, in the product, and on calls. None of them are complicated once you have the right picture in your head.

Objective
One single thing a person needs to learn or prove they can do. Think of it as one line on a checklist, for example "can this person safely run this machine." An Objective can require a video, a quiz, a certain number of supervised hours, or a trainer's sign-off before it counts as done.
Objective Group
A bundle of Objectives, like a folder holding several checklist items together. For example, "Certified Level 2 Machinist" might bundle five separate Objectives. If even one item inside expires or was never completed, the whole folder shows as "not qualified" until it's fixed. Think of a video game "achievement" that only unlocks once you have finished every smaller task inside it.
Skills Matrix
The master scoreboard. A big grid where the rows are people and the columns are Objectives or Objective Groups, color coded green, yellow, or red, so a supervisor can look at one screen and instantly see who is ready for what.
Process Control (gating)
A guardrail that plugs into the shop floor system and physically blocks someone from being assigned or signing off on a job they aren't qualified for. Like a bouncer checking IDs, but the door is a specific task on the production line.
Performance-based Proficiency
Being qualified is not a one time checkbox. If someone hasn't actually done a job in a while, Covalent can flag that they need a refresher. Like a driver's license: you technically still have it, but if you haven't driven in two years, you're probably rusty.
Coverage Planning
A staffing forecast. It answers "do we have enough qualified people to hit our production target by a certain date, and where are we short?"
Workforce Availability
Knowing not just who is qualified on paper, but who is actually clocked in right now, so a supervisor can plug a gap immediately instead of guessing.
Renewal
Like a license or certification, Objectives can expire and need to be redone on a schedule to stay current.

Covalent is a growing enterprise SaaS company selling into complex manufacturing environments, especially aerospace and defense, automotive, and industrial. As of the FY2027 plan, the company is scaling from roughly $5.0M in starting ARR toward a $7.7M year end target, adding a small, high leverage go to market team along the way. Why Covalent exists

Manufacturers are losing their most experienced workers to retirement faster than they can train replacements. At the same time, customers like Boeing and regulators like the FAA and DCMA expect airtight, auditable proof that every worker on a job was actually qualified to do it. Paper and spreadsheets cannot keep up with either problem. Covalent exists to close that gap.

Understand the Product

Go one level deeper than the simple version so you can hold a real conversation about how the platform actually works.

The core workflow, start to finish

  1. A trainee is assigned one or more Objectives or Objective Groups, individually or in bulk (for example, everyone in a location or role).
  2. The trainee works through the Objective: watching content, completing checklists, logging hours or parts, and being observed or signed off by a trainer or evaluator.
  3. Once complete, the Objective (and any parent Objective Group) shows as qualified on the Skills Matrix in real time.
  4. If the role requires it, Covalent's Process Control integration with the customer's MES can gate or flag work if an unqualified person is assigned to it.
  5. Over time, Objectives can expire or require Renewal, and Performance-based Proficiency can flag someone as rusty if they haven't actually done the job recently.

Capabilities worth knowing at your level

  • Skills Matrix: real time, filterable view of who is qualified for what, across a whole workforce.
  • Coverage Planning: helps leaders answer "do we have enough trained people to hit our production target."
  • Immutable audit trail: every action is time stamped and signed, which is exactly what auditors from the FAA or DCMA ask for.
  • Integrations: Covalent connects to MES, LMS, HRIS, and SSO systems rather than replacing them.
You do not need to be a product expert as an SDR. You need enough fluency to ask a smart discovery question and recognize when a prospect's pain matches something Covalent solves. Save the deep technical detail for the AE and the demo.

Learn the Sales Process

Understand where your job starts and ends, and exactly how a lead flows from first touch to a booked, qualified meeting.

Your role in the funnel

As an SDR or BDR, you own the top of the funnel. Your job is to generate outbound conversations, qualify interest, and hand off clean, well documented meetings to an Account Executive. You are not expected to run a demo or negotiate a deal.

The outbound cadence

  1. Research: confirm the account fits the ICP (aerospace/defense, automotive, or complex discrete manufacturing) and identify the right persona.
  2. Multi-touch outreach: combine email, LinkedIn, and phone across a sequence rather than a single touch. Reference the Closed-Lost Re-Engagement Campaign template for a full example of a structured, multi-step sequence.
  3. Qualify: confirm there is a real pain (paper or spreadsheet based tracking, audit exposure, staffing visibility gaps), a relevant role, and rough timing.
  4. Book the meeting: lock in a discovery call with the right persona and pass it to an AE.
  5. Handoff: log clear, complete notes so the AE walks into the call prepared, not cold.

A simple qualification bar

CheckWhat you're confirming
PainThey currently rely on paper, spreadsheets, or a system that doesn't talk to their MES/LMS for qualification tracking
FitThey are in an ICP industry and are a manufacturer of meaningful scale
AuthorityYou are talking to, or can get to, someone with real influence: training, ops, quality, or digital/IT leadership
TimingThere is a reason to act soon: an audit cycle, a ramp, a new program, or a leadership mandate

What a clean handoff looks like

  • Persona and title of the contact
  • The specific pain they described, in their own words if possible
  • Any objections already raised and how you addressed them
  • Confirmed meeting time and who from the customer side will attend
Milestone check: Successfully book and hand off a meeting where the AE tells you the notes were clear enough that they didn't need to ask you a single follow up question.

Master the Messaging

Have the exact language ready so you never fumble the "what does Covalent do" moment or a common objection.

The one-liner

"We help industrial companies manage training, qualification, and assignment of work at scale. We replace paper, Excel and tribal knowledge with a modern system to track who's qualified to work on what."

Follow with: "We ramp new operators about 30% faster and save each worker around 10 minutes of unproductive time per shift. Worth a quick call to see how it compares to what you're doing today?"

Call to action ladder, lightest first

  1. "Can I send over a 2-minute video on how the platform works?"
  2. "Would you be open to a quick benchmarking call, 20 minutes to compare how peers are handling this?"
  3. "How does [Company] currently manage whether a technician is qualified for a specific job?" (a question hook, great for getting them talking)
  4. "We'll be in [city] next month implementing at [Boeing/GE site], worth grabbing 20 minutes while we're there?"

8

Develop Role-Specific Skills

Build the muscle memory that separates a good SDR from a great one.

High-volume prospecting
Build and work a full pipeline of accounts daily, not just the easy ones. Practice: build a target list of 25 accounts in one ICP segment and prioritize by fit.
Cold calling fundamentals
Confident opens, a clear reason for the call, and a genuine question that invites a real answer. Practice: record yourself doing five practice cold calls and review with your manager.
Email and LinkedIn outreach
Write outreach that reads like a person wrote it, not a template. Practice: draft a 3-email sequence for a specific persona and get feedback before sending live.
Qualification
Quickly and respectfully confirm pain, fit, authority, and timing on a live call. Practice: role-play a qualification call with a peer playing a skeptical prospect.
Objection handling
Stay calm and curious rather than defensive when you hear "we already use an LMS" or "not the right time." Practice: drill the three core objections until the response is automatic.
Booking meetings
Lock in a specific time, confirm attendees, and set expectations for what the call will cover. Practice: book five meetings and track show rate.
Clean CRM hygiene
Every touch, note, and status update logged accurately and on time. Practice: have your manager audit a week of your CRM activity for completeness.
Effective handoff to AEs
Give the AE everything they need so the first real sales conversation feels like a continuation, not a cold start. Practice: get direct feedback from an AE after your first three handoffs.
Milestone check: Your manager observes you complete one full cycle, prospecting through handoff, and signs off that each skill above was demonstrated.
9

Prove Competency

Demonstrate you're ready to run outbound independently before you're handed a full pipeline.

Certification checklist

  • Deliver the Covalent one-liner and all three core objection responses from memory
  • Explain an Objective, an Objective Group, and the Skills Matrix in your own words
  • Pass a mock cold call reviewed by your manager
  • Shadow at least three live calls with a senior SDR or AE
  • Book your first live meeting with a manager listening in or reviewing the recording
  • Demonstrate clean CRM hygiene across one full week of activity
  • Complete a clean handoff confirmed as sufficient by the receiving AE
  • Manager sign-off to move to full independent ramp
Milestone check: All boxes above checked and signed off by your manager before entering Stage 10.

Ramp into full production with a safety net, not on your own.

PhaseFocus

Days 31-60Run your own outbound cadence with regular call reviews, ramp toward a partial activity and meeting target

Targets (meetings booked per week, calls per day, sequences enrolled per week) are set by your manager based on current team benchmarks. Ask for these numbers on day one and revisit them at each ramp milestone.
